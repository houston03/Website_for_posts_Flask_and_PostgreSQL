More about the neural network

A neural network (also an artificial neural network, INS, or simply a neural network) is a mathematical model, as well as its software or hardware embodiment, built on the principle of organizing biological neural networks â€” networks of nerve cells of a living organism. This concept arose when studying the processes occurring in the brain, and when trying to simulate these processes. The first such attempt was the neural networks of W. McCulloch and W. Pitts. After the development of learning algorithms, the resulting models began to be used for practical purposes: in forecasting tasks, for pattern recognition, in management tasks, etc.
